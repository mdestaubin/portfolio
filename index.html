As a mission-driven designer and researcher, I've dedicated my career to collaborating with cross-disciplinary teams in public health and humanitarian contexts, while leveraging my design perspective and skillsets

My journey began in architecture where I was fortunate to work on high-impact projects in low-income settings that aimed to transform the lives of individual families and entire communities. However, a profound experience working with an NGO client during the 2014-2016 Ebola Outbreak in West Africa inspired me to return to school to explore how I could broaden and apply my design background and skillset within the humanitarian field as a full-time career.

Since graduating in 2018, I've had the incredible opportunity to cultivate my profressional growth at the Harvard Humanitarian Initiative, where I've supported a diverse array of humanitarian research and operational programs. While my interests and skillsets remain broad, I've developed a particular skill and interest in building data collection and management systems with real-time, interactive data monitoring, and analysis dashboards. The applications for these dashboards have been diverse, ranging from disease surveillance platforms for the CDC and the Dominican Republic's Ministry of Health, to user registration and knowledge retention dashboards tracking key metrics for a global humanitarian response online training program.
